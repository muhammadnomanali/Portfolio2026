Car Safety Infographic

Visual guide explaining vehicle structural safety for informed car buying decisions.

Back to Artifacts

This infographic project emerged from research into how consumers interpret vehicle safety information. Many buyers struggle to understand the technical aspects of car safety, relying instead on marketing claims and superficial metrics. The goal was to create a visual guide that makes safety features and structural design principles understandable to a general audience, enabling more informed purchasing decisions.

Key Concepts Explained

Rigid Passenger Safety Cell

The cabin structure maintains its shape during impact, protecting occupants from crushing forces and structural collapse.

Controlled Crumple Zones

The front and rear sections deform in a controlled manner, absorbing impact energy before it reaches passengers.

Impulse-Momentum Principle

Extended deformation time reduces the force experienced by occupants, following the physics principle F = Δp/Δt.

Pillar Integrity

Reinforced A, B, and C pillars maintain roof height and door frame rigidity during rollover and side-impact scenarios.

Design Process

  1. 1
    Research Phase: Studied vehicle safety standards (NHTSA, IIHS) and analyzed consumer understanding gaps through literature review.
  2. 2
    Information Architecture: Identified four core concepts critical to vehicle safety and determined the logical flow for explaining each component.
  3. 3
    Visual Design: Created illustrations using color-coding and cross-sections to simplify complex structural elements and force diagrams.
  4. 4
    Refinement & Testing: Gathered feedback from non-technical users and iteratively improved clarity and visual hierarchy.
